CLOSE-UP
📹 Docufiction ⏱ 98 min

Close-Up

Directed by Abbas Kiarostami

A mesmerizing blend of documentary and fiction following Hossain Sabzian, who impersonated filmmaker Mohsen Makhmalbaf. A profound meditation on identity, art, and class in post-revolutionary Iran.

Cannes — FIPRESCI Prize Sight & Sound — Top 10

JODAI-E NADER
🎭 Drama ⏱ 123 min

A Separation

Directed by Asghar Farhadi

A middle-class couple in Tehran faces a wrenching decision about leaving Iran for the sake of their daughter. When the husband hires a lower-class caregiver for his elderly father, tragedy strikes and legal complications ensue.

Academy Award — Best Foreign Film Cannes — Best Actress Golden Bear Nominee
1997

TA'M E GUILASS
🎭 Drama ⏱ 95 min

Taste of Cherry

Directed by Abbas Kiarostami

Mr. Badii drives through the hills outside Tehran, searching for someone to assist him in a desperate act. A minimalist masterpiece about life, death, and the simple pleasures that make existence worthwhile.

Cannes — Palme d'Or César — Best Foreign Film
2000

DAYEREH
🎭 Drama ⏱ 90 min

The Circle

Directed by Jafar Panahi

Banned in Iran, this urgent drama follows several women navigating the streets of Tehran after release from prison. A searing indictment of patriarchal oppression and one of the most important feminist films of the 21st century.

Venice — Golden Lion FIPRESCI Prize UNESCO Award
1987

KHANEH-YE
🎭 Drama ⏱ 83 min

Where Is the Friend's House?

Directed by Abbas Kiarostami

A young boy must return his friend's notebook to prevent him from being expelled. Shot in the earthquake-ravaged village of Koker, this gentle tale of childhood responsibility became the foundation of the Koker Trilogy.

Locarno — Bronze Leopard FIPRESCI Prize
2016

FORUSHANDE
🎭 Thriller ⏱ 124 min

The Salesman

Directed by Asghar Farhadi

A couple's relationship turns violent after they move into a new apartment with a dark history. As they perform Arthur Miller's "Death of a Salesman," the boundaries between performance and reality dissolve.

🎭 Drama ⏱ 104 min

The Cow

Directed by Dariush Mehrjui

The foundational film of the Iranian New Wave. When a villager's beloved cow dies, he descends into madness, believing he has become the animal. A powerful allegory of dignity, loss, and rural transformation.

Directed by Marjane Satrapi & Vincent Paronnaud

Based on Satrapi's autobiographical graphic novel, this animated masterpiece follows a young girl coming of age during the Islamic Revolution and Iran-Iraq War. A poignant tale of exile, identity, and resilience.

DIRECTORS

Visionaries who shaped Iranian cinema and influenced filmmakers worldwide

Abbas Kiarostami

1940 — 2016

Poetic minimalism, blurred boundaries between documentary and fiction, humanist philosophy, and the Koker Trilogy.

Close-Up, Taste of Cherry, The Wind Will Carry Us, Certified Copy

Asghar Farhadi

1972 — Present

Social thrillers exploring morality, class tension, and family dissolution through intricate narrative puzzles.

A Separation, The Salesman, A Hero, About Elly

Jafar Panahi

1960 — Present

Defiant neo-realism, banned from filmmaking yet continues to create urgent works about oppression and resistance.

The Circle, The White Balloon, Taxi, No Bears

Majid Majidi

1959 — Present

Spiritual neorealism focusing on children, poverty, and transcendence through everyday struggles.

Children of Heaven, The Color of Paradise, Baran, The Song of Sparrows

Dariush Mehrjui

1939 — 2023

Intellectual cinema adapting Western literature to Iranian contexts, founder of the New Wave movement.

The Cow, The Cycle, Hamoun, Sara

Sohrab Shahid Saless

1944 — 1998

Minimalist long takes, static camera work, and contemplative studies of time and alienation.

A Simple Event, Still Life, The Journey

Forugh Farrokhzad

1935 — 1967

Poetic cinema, feminist icon, tragically short career but monumental influence with one masterpiece.

The House is Black (1962)

Mohsen Makhmalbaf

1957 — Present

Politically engaged cinema evolving from Islamic revolutionary themes to poetic international co-productions.

The Cyclist, Gabbeh, Kandahar, The Silence

TIMELINE

1969
The Iranian New Wave Begins
Dariush Mehrjui's The Cow premieres, establishing intellectual, socially critical cinema distinct from mainstream Iranian film.
1979
The Islamic Revolution
Cinema faces severe restrictions. Many directors go into exile. The industry nearly collapses but eventually adapts to new codes.
1987
Kiarostami's Breakthrough
Where Is the Friend's House? wins international acclaim, beginning the global recognition of post-revolutionary Iranian cinema.
1990
Close-Up
Abbas Kiarostami's genre-defying masterpiece cements Iranian cinema's reputation for philosophical innovation.
1997
Palme d'Or for Iran
Kiarostami's Taste of Cherry wins the Palme d'Or at Cannes, the highest honor in world cinema.
2000
The Circle & Repression
Jafar Panahi wins the Golden Lion at Venice, but his film is banned in Iran, beginning his conflict with authorities.
2011
Oscar Victory
Asghar Farhadi's A Separation becomes the first Iranian film to win the Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film.
2015
Panahi Defies Ban
Despite a 20-year filmmaking ban, Jafar Panahi creates Taxi, winning the Golden Bear at Berlin.
2022
Woman, Life, Freedom
Cinema becomes a tool of resistance during nationwide protests. Directors face increased persecution but continue creating.
